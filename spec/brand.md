# Gitcoin Brand Overview

This document summarizes the Gitcoin brand aesthetic, philosophy, and color system.
It is intended for engineers, designers, and contributors building Gitcoin-aligned products, interfaces, or experiments.

The goal of the Gitcoin brand is coherence without rigidity.
Gitcoin is designed as a living ecosystem, not a static corporate identity.

---

## Brand Philosophy

Gitcoin represents public goods funding as an ecological and cultural system.

Core ideas expressed through the brand:
- coordination over extraction
- ecosystems over platforms
- community over corporation
- regeneration over optimization
- long-term stewardship over short-term gains

The visual identity intentionally avoids cold enterprise aesthetics.
It favors organic, networked, and human-centered design cues.

The brand supports experimentation, plurality, and evolution while remaining recognizably Gitcoin.

---

## Visual Identity Principles

- wordmark-first identity
- simple, modern typography
- high legibility over decoration
- soft backgrounds and restrained accents
- biomimetic and nature-inspired metaphors
- flexibility across products and contexts

Icons, illustrations, and textures should feel organic, not mechanical.
Gradients are atmospheric, not noisy.
Visual density stays low to medium.

---

## Color System Overview

Gitcoin does not use a single fixed palette.
Instead, it operates with mode-based color systems that map to different narratives and use cases.

Pick one mode per surface or experience.
Do not mix modes arbitrarily.

Accents are used sparingly to guide attention.

---

## Lichenpunk Mode (Core Default)

Organic, ecological, community-first.
This is the most Gitcoin-native palette.

### Primary
- Moss Green: `#6F8F72`
- Deep Lichen: `#4F6F64`
- Teal Green: `#4AAE9B`

### Neutrals
- Off White: `#F5F7F4`
- Warm Gray: `#D9DED8`
- Slate Gray: `#7A847F`

### Accents
- Gitcoin Green: `#00EB88`
- Soft Cyan: `#6AE3C2`

**Use for**
- grants
- governance
- community surfaces
- ecosystem storytelling

## Text and Contrast

### Light Backgrounds
- Primary Text: `#0F172A`
- Secondary Text: `#475569`

### Dark Backgrounds
- Primary Text: `#F8FAFC`
- Secondary Text: `#CBD5E1`

Accessibility and readability always take priority over expression.

---

## Usage Guidelines

- choose one color mode per surface
- keep backgrounds soft and breathable
- use accents to guide attention, not decorate
- avoid visual noise and excessive contrast
- prioritize clarity, calm, and trust

If something feels corporate, over-designed, or salesy, it is probably off-brand.
